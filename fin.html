PSA SUMMARY

Methods & Transparency

Eligibility Criteria (PICOS)

Population: Adults with cardiorenal disease, including diabetic kidney disease (DKD), chronic kidney disease (CKD stages 2-4), heart failure (HFrEF, HFpEF, HFmrEF), post-myocardial infarction with LV dysfunction, and type 2 diabetes with established cardiovascular disease or risk factors.

Intervention: Finerenone (non-steroidal MRA), spironolactone or eplerenone (steroidal MRA), SGLT2 inhibitors (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin, canagliflozin, ertugliflozin) or dual SGLT1/2 inhibitor (sotagliflozin).

Comparator: Placebo or standard of care (including background RASi therapy).

Outcomes: Cardiovascular composite (CV death + heart failure hospitalization), renal composite (kidney failure, sustained eGFR decline, or renal death ± CV death), and all-cause mortality.

Study design: Phase III-IV randomized controlled trials with published hazard ratios or rate ratios and 95% confidence intervals. Minimum N ≥ 1,000 randomized participants.

Statistical Methods

Meta-analysis: Stratified DerSimonian-Laird (DL) random-effects via weighted least squares (WLS) with one-hot drug-class design matrix (class-level pooling vs. shared placebo comparator). This is not a network meta-analysis (NMA) or component NMA — all comparisons are direct (active vs. placebo) with no indirect evidence. The term "class effects" denotes stratified pooling within pre-specified drug classes. Confidence intervals use t-distribution (not z). Prediction intervals follow IntHout/Higgins/Rover (2016) with per-class df = kj - 2. Heterogeneity reported as residual within-class I2 and Cochran Q.

DL limitations & REML sensitivity: DerSimonian-Laird has known downward bias for small k (<10), producing narrower CIs than REML or Paule-Mandel. Per-class trial counts range from 2-10 in this analysis. CRES provides built-in REML + Hartung-Knapp-Sidik-Jonkman (HKSJ) sensitivity analysis alongside the primary DL estimates. REML tau2 is estimated via golden section search on the profile restricted log-likelihood for one-hot design matrices. HKSJ inflates variance by max(1, Qw/(k-p)) and uses t-distribution critical values, producing wider CIs that better reflect estimation uncertainty when k is small. Both DL and REML tau2 estimates are displayed in the panel header; full REML+HKSJ CIs are available in the screen-reader results. DL remains the primary estimator for computational transparency, consistent with Cochrane defaults. A shared tau2 is estimated across all classes; class-specific heterogeneity may differ.

I2 interpretation: Within-class I2 reflects residual heterogeneity after accounting for drug-class stratification. I2 depends on study precision and should not be compared across analyses with different study sizes. Low I2 does not imply clinical homogeneity, particularly when pooling across distinct indications (DKD, HFrEF, HFpEF). Cochran Q p-values have low power for k < 10.

PCA: SVD-based eigendecomposition on Z-score standardized features [Age, %Male, eGFR, Year]. Distance metric: L2 in full 4-dimensional PC space. Caveat: With N=20 trials and p=4 features, the covariance matrix is estimated from a small sample. PCA loadings and explained variance should be interpreted as descriptive geometry, not inferential. Year is a secular trend variable capturing changes in trial design over time, not a patient-level clinical characteristic.

Publication bias: Egger's unweighted OLS regression of standardized effects on precision (1/SE), with t-distribution p-value (df = n - 2). Limitation: This test uses raw study-level HRs, not class-residualized effects; systematic differences between drug classes may confound the bias assessment. A class-stratified Egger's test or per-class funnel plots would provide more interpretable results but require larger per-class sample sizes.

Simulation Model

Structure: 3-state competing-risks Markov model (Stable CKD → ESRD → Dead), quarterly cycles, 10-year horizon, half-cycle correction (Sonnenberg-Beck). Discount rate: 3.0% (Second Panel on Cost-Effectiveness in Health and Medicine).

Parameters: Base mortality 6%/yr (Go et al. 2004, CKD Stage 3 average). Important limitation: HF populations have substantially higher mortality rates (15-25%/yr); the blended 6%/yr rate underestimates mortality for HF-enriched bins. A scenario analysis with higher base mortality (e.g., 15%/yr for HF subgroups) would better characterize uncertainty. Population-specific Markov arms with indication-stratified transition rates are recommended for future development. ESRD mortality multiplier: 3x. Drug costs: per-class (Finerenone $8,500/yr US Kerendia; SGLT2i $6,000/yr; Spironolactone $150/yr). ESRD cost: $90,000/yr (USRDS 2024). Utilities: stable CKD 0.85 (Sullivan 2011), ESRD 0.58 (Gorodetskaya 2005).

PSA: 1,000 Monte Carlo iterations with seeded PRNG (xoshiro128**, seed=42). Distributions: Beta (rates, utilities), Gamma (costs with per-class sampling at CV=18%), Lognormal (HRs with predictive variance including between-study tau2). CEAC via net monetary benefit criterion (pairwise vs. Status Quo). Simplification: Mortality and renal HRs are sampled independently in each PSA iteration; in practice, these outcomes are correlated within trials (treatment affects both endpoints). This independence assumption may underestimate joint parameter uncertainty but is standard for sensitivity analyses with separate outcome-specific meta-analyses.

Reporting Standards

PRISMA 2020: This tool presents a curated registry of 20 landmark cardiorenal trials. A trial selection flow diagram is provided below (see "Trial Selection Flow"). While not a formal systematic review with comprehensive search strategy, the inclusion criteria are transparent and reproducible.

Risk of Bias: Cochrane RoB 2.0 assessment for all 20 included trials is provided below (see "Risk of Bias 2.0"). Key concerns: TOPCAT (high risk for D1-Randomization due to regional recruitment heterogeneity and low spironolactone metabolite levels at non-Americas sites; de Denus et al. NEJM 2017), SCORED and SOLOIST-WHF (some concerns for D3-Missing data due to early termination from COVID-19/funding loss).

GRADE: Certainty-of-evidence assessment using the GRADE framework (Guyatt et al., BMJ 2008;336:924-926) is provided below for all 3 outcomes (see "GRADE Certainty of Evidence"). GRADE considers both downgrade factors (risk of bias, inconsistency, indirectness, imprecision, publication bias) and upgrade factors (large effect, dose-response, plausible confounding). For the composite outcome, large effect magnitude (HR<0.80 for some classes) could support an upgrade, but cross-indication heterogeneity and the exploratory nature of this synthesis argue against upgrading at this stage.

REML Sensitivity: DerSimonian-Laird estimates are accompanied by REML + Hartung-Knapp-Sidik-Jonkman (HKSJ) sensitivity analysis, displayed in the tau2 header and screen-reader results.

Registration: No PROSPERO protocol was prospectively registered, as this is an interactive evidence synthesis tool rather than a formal systematic review. Future iterations with prospective update protocols should consider PROSPERO registration to align with living review best practices (Thomas J et al., J Clin Epidemiol 2017).

Data Availability

All trial-level data (hazard ratios, confidence intervals, sample sizes, population features) are hardcoded in the source code of this HTML file (CONFIG.trials object, viewable via browser "View Source"). All values are extracted from published peer-reviewed manuscripts and ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT IDs provided; RALES predates ClinicalTrials.gov and uses the key RALES-1999). No individual patient data is used. This is a secondary analysis of published aggregate data; no ethical approval was required per institutional guidelines.

Data provenance: Each trial entry in CONFIG.trials includes the NCT ID, sample size (randomized N), drug class, population label, and outcome-level hazard ratios with 95% CIs extracted from primary publications. The estimand field distinguishes hazard ratios (hr) from rate ratios (rr). The renalDef field tracks whether renal composites include CV death. Feature vectors [Age, %Male, eGFR, Year] are extracted from baseline characteristics tables. Sources are annotated in note fields within the CONFIG object.

Computational Validation

TruthCert: CRES uses cryptographic fingerprinting (SHA-256 via Web Crypto API) to detect data tampering. The evidence fingerprint covers CONFIG.trials data. The "Verify" button re-runs all meta-analyses from frozen CONFIG data and compares result hashes against a stored reference. A "VERIFIED" result confirms that trial data and engine computations are unchanged from the sealed version.

Determinism: All stochastic computations (PSA Monte Carlo) use a seeded PRNG (xoshiro128**, seed=42), ensuring bitwise-identical results across runs and platforms. The Selenium test suite validates all statistical outputs against expected values.

Cross-validation: DerSimonian-Laird and REML pooled estimates were independently cross-validated against R metafor v4.8 (rma(yi, sei, mods=~factor(class)-1, method="DL"/"REML", test="knha")). All class-level HRs, tau2 estimates, and I2 matched to ≥3 decimal places: composite DL tau2=0.0047, REML tau2=0.0046, I2=50.3%; renal DL/REML tau2≈0 (homogeneous); mortality DL tau2=0.0088, REML tau2=0.009, I2=57.4%. HKSJ CIs from metafor were systematically wider than DL CIs, confirming expected behavior with small per-class k. The stratified WLS implementation with one-hot design matrix produces identical point estimates to standard IV-weighted fixed-effect pooling when tau2=0. Chi-squared p-values use exact solutions for df=1-2 and Wilson-Hilferty approximation for df≥3. The R validation script is embedded below (View Source, search for validate_metafor).

Guideline Context

ESC 2023: The 2023 ESC Focused Update on Heart Failure (McDonagh et al., Eur Heart J 2023;44:3627-3639) gives MRA therapy a Class I recommendation for HFrEF and gives finerenone a Class I recommendation for DKD with T2DM (Section 11.4). KDIGO 2024 CKD guideline update (KDIGO 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for CKD, Kidney Int 2024;105(4S):S117-S314) recommends a layered approach: first-line RASi + SGLT2i, then consider finerenone if albuminuria persists. This tool enables interactive exploration of trial-level evidence supporting these recommendations.

Interactive Evidence Synthesis: CRES implements a browser-based, zero-installation cumulative meta-analysis visualization for cardiorenal pharmacotherapy. The cumulative meta-analysis feature is inspired by living systematic review methods (Thomas J et al., J Clin Epidemiol 2017;91:31-37; Cochrane Living Reviews) but CRES does not constitute a living review: it lacks pre-specified update triggers, stopping rules, formal evidence stabilization assessment, and a prospective update protocol. CRES is an interactive evidence synthesis tool, not a living systematic review. It provides a transparent, reproducible tool for visualizing how pooled estimates evolve chronologically, enabling rapid evidence assessment without requiring server infrastructure.

Cumulative Evidence Visualization

Cumulative Meta-Analysis: Trials are ordered chronologically by publication year. At each step k, the IV-weighted pooled hazard ratio is computed using DerSimonian-Laird random-effects with t-distribution CIs (df = k-1). At k=1, the normal quantile is used as the t-distribution with 0 df is degenerate; this single-study estimate should be interpreted with extreme caution. Trials reporting rate ratios (FINEARTS-HF, SCORED, SOLOIST-WHF) are excluded from IV-weighted pooling due to incompatible estimands.

Cardiorenal Correlation: For trials reporting both cardiac and renal HR outcomes, a scatter plot displays cardiac HR vs. renal HR. Currently only 2 trials qualify (both Finerenone/DKD); this visualization becomes more informative as dual-outcome trials accumulate across drug classes. When fewer than 3 trials or fewer than 2 drug classes qualify, a sparsity warning is displayed.

Evidence Timeline: An interactive timeline displays trials by publication year and drug class, providing a chronological overview of the cardiorenal evidence landscape from RALES (1999) to FINEARTS-HF (2024).

Patient-Facing Mode

Traffic-light thresholds: The patient-mode visualization uses HR thresholds to categorize treatments: green (HR < 0.80, "Strong benefit shown"), amber (HR 0.80-0.95, "Moderate benefit shown"), red (HR ≥ 0.95, "Benefit uncertain"). These thresholds are heuristic cutoffs chosen to convey relative effect magnitude in lay terms; they are NOT derived from clinical guidelines or minimum clinically important differences. The green threshold of 0.80 roughly corresponds to a 20% relative risk reduction, commonly considered a clinically meaningful effect in cardiovascular trials. These categories are for communication purposes only and do not replace clinical judgment.

Limitations

Composite endpoint definitions vary across trials (4p-MACE+HHF, CV death+HHF, kidney composite ± CV death). Renal composite definitions differ materially: SGLT2i trials (DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY, CREDENCE) include CV death in the renal composite, while finerenone trials use kidney-only endpoints — this may dilute the SGLT2i renal-specific signal (adding a non-renal component) rather than inflate it, complicating direct comparison with finerenone kidney-only endpoints. Populations are clinically heterogeneous (DKD, HFrEF, HFpEF, post-MI, T2DM+CVD); indication-level subgroup analyses (e.g., CKD-only, HF-only) would provide more clinically actionable estimates but reduce per-class sample sizes. Sotagliflozin is a dual SGLT1/2 inhibitor grouped with SGLT2i for pragmatic class-level analysis (SGLT1 inhibition affects GLP-1 and postprandial glucose). TOPCAT overall result is pooled (not Americas subgroup) as a conservative ITT approach. The trial registry includes 20 curated trials; the CANVAS Program 3p-MACE result is excluded from composite pooling (different endpoint definition) but mortality data are included. Health economic parameters are US-centric (per-class drug costs: Finerenone $8,500/yr, SGLT2i $6,000/yr, Spironolactone $150/yr; US WTP thresholds, 3.0% discount rate); European cost perspectives differ substantially (e.g., UK NICE TA877 uses NHS list prices; German GBA uses AMNOG-negotiated prices); WTP thresholds also vary internationally (UK: £20,000-30,000/QALY; US: $50,000-150,000/QALY). Risk-Based Allocation uses an exploratory eGFR < 45 threshold without cited evidence basis; SGLT2i trials demonstrate efficacy to eGFR 20 (EMPA-KIDNEY, DAPA-CKD). Base mortality rate (6%/yr) underestimates mortality in HF subgroups (15-25%/yr). Leave-one-out analysis requires k ≥ 3 studies per class to be informative (omitting 1 of 2 studies is trivially the remaining study).
